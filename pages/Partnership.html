[1Chronicles 29:3-9]

3 Moreover, because I have set my affection on the house of my God, I have given to the house of my God, over and above all that I have prepared for the holy house, my own special treasure of gold and silver: 4 three thousand talents of gold, of the gold of Ophir, and seven thousand talents of refined silver, to overlay the walls of the houses; 5 the gold for [things of] gold and the silver for [things of] silver, and for all kinds of work [to be done] by the hands of craftsmen. Who [then] is willing to consecrate himself this day to the LORD?" 6 Then the leaders of the fathers' [houses,] leaders of the tribes of Israel, the captains of thousands and of hundreds, with the officers over the king's work, offered willingly. 7 They gave for the work of the house of God five thousand talents and ten thousand darics of gold, ten thousand talents of silver, eighteen thousand talents of bronze, and one hundred thousand talents of iron. 8 And whoever had [precious] stones gave [them] to the treasury of the house of the LORD, into the hand of Jehiel the Gershonite. 9 Then the people rejoiced, for they had offered willingly, because with a loyal heart they had offered willingly to the LORD; and King David also rejoiced greatly.

The above portion of scriptures shows how the people were giving towards the work of the Lord which was building the house of the Lord. Verse 9 shows how people rejoiced for they had offered willingly to the Lord. Note: The scripture says that the people rejoiced because they offered willingly .

[2Corinthians 9:6]

But this I say to you he who sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and he who sows bountifully will also reap bountifully. So let each one give as he purposes in his heart, not grudgingly or of necessity for God loves a cheerful giver.

In both accounts, they gave with a loyal heart, not grudgingly or reluctantly. When giving to the Lord or towards the work of the Lord, never do it in complaint and never lose courage when giving, for we know that it's the kingdom pattern unto a sustained flow of the blessing of God towards us so we should willingly, joyfully and cheerfully do it because as children of God we know is the only way we can extract or draw out every blessing that we carry in the inside of us into manifestation, so giving should always be seen as increase time and harvest time not as though you are losing what you're giving. 8 And God is able to make all grace abound towards you, that you always having all sufficiency in all things, may have an abundance for every good work.

As a result of giving with such revelation, you tap into the ever abounding sufficient treasuries of God.
